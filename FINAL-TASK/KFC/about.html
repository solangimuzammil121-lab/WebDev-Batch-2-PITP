Our Story

From a small service station to the world's most popular chicken restaurant

The KFC Story

In 1930, during the Great Depression, Harland Sanders opened his first restaurant in the front room of a gas station in Corbin, Kentucky. Sanders served as station operator, chief cook, and cashier, and named the dining area "Sanders Court & Caf√©."

In 1936, Sanders was made an honorary Kentucky Colonel in recognition of his contributions to the state's cuisine. In 1952, he began franchising his chicken business, and the name "Kentucky Fried Chicken" was born.

Today, KFC is a global brand with over 24,000 restaurants in more than 145 countries. We serve nearly 12 million customers each day, all thanks to Colonel Sanders' secret recipe of 11 herbs and spices.
